Your 12 Rights

Know Your Rights as a Patient

Hover over each card to read what the right means for your care at St. Nicholas.

01

Right to Information

Right to relevant information in a language and manner the patient understands, including diagnosis, treatment, and possible outcomes.

You have the right to know exactly what is happening with your health. Your doctor must explain your diagnosis and plan in your preferred language where possible.

02

Right to Medical Records

Right of timely access to detailed and accurate medical records and available services.

You may request access to your medical records at any time. Our team will provide accurate, up-to-date information about your health history.

03

Right to Transparent Billing

Right to transparent billing and full disclosure of any cost, including recommended treatment plans.

You will always be informed of costs before they are incurred. We provide detailed estimates and itemised bills — no hidden charges.

04

Right to Privacy & Confidentiality

Right to privacy, and confidentiality of medical records.

Your medical information is strictly confidential. It is shared only with members of your direct care team.

05

Right to a Safe Environment

Right to a clean, safe and secure healthcare environment.

You have the right to receive care in a clean, hygienic, and safe facility maintained to international standards.

06

Right to Respectful Treatment

Right to be treated with respect, regardless of gender, race, religion, ethnicity, or disability.

Every patient deserves dignity and respect. Our staff are committed to treating all patients equally, without discrimination.

07

Right to Emergency Care

Right to receive urgent, immediate and sufficient intervention in the event of an emergency.

In a medical emergency, you have the right to be seen immediately, regardless of your ability to pay at that moment.

08

Right to Visitation

Right to reasonable visitation in accordance with prevailing rules and regulations.

You have the right to receive visitors during designated hours. The clinical team will keep you and your family informed.

09

Right to Decline Care

Right to decline care, subject to laws and upon full disclosure of the consequences.

You may refuse or stop any treatment after being fully informed. Your decision will be respected and documented.

10

Right to Decline Research Participation

Right to decline or consent to participation in medical research or clinical trials.

You will never be enrolled in research without your explicit written consent. You may withdraw at any time.

11

Right to Quality Care

Right to quality care in accordance with prevailing standards.

You are entitled to care that meets national and international standards. We are COHSASA-accredited.

12

Right to Complain

Right to complain and express dissatisfaction regarding services received.

If you are unhappy with any aspect of your care, you have the right to raise a formal complaint without penalty.

Raising a Concern

How to Make a Complaint

You have the right to express dissatisfaction. Here is how to do it.

1
Speak to the Nurse in Charge

Most issues can be resolved quickly at this level.

2
Contact Patient Relations

3
Formal Written Complaint

Written complaints receive a response within 14 working days.

4
Escalate to the CPC
