Accident & Emergency Unit

The Accident and Emergency Unit of St. Nicholas Hospital assesses and treats people with serious injuries, sudden severe illness or a medical emergency. It is open 24 hours a day, 365 days of the year.

An air-conditioned out-patient treatment room is provided, equipped with a modern operating table and anaesthetic apparatus. The unit also has an Intensive Therapy area for resuscitation of emergency cases — particularly for babies and accident victims.

Ambulance Services

Dialysis Unit

The Dialysis Unit of St. Nicholas Hospital is dedicated to improving health outcomes for patients with end-stage renal disease (ESRD). We operate one of the busiest privately-owned dialysis centres in Lagos.

Dialysis is a medical procedure in which the functions of a kidney — removing metabolic waste and excess water — are taken over by a machine. This may be done temporarily (days or weeks) or over a long period (years). With 15,000 new cases of kidney failure occurring every year in Nigeria, dialysis is a critical service.

Our Unit

10-bed dedicated dialysis unitSeparate ICU dialysis unit for critically ill 6 days per week service24-hour on-call for emergencies Home dialysis support programme

Our team includes specialist nephrology nurses with extensive renal nursing experience, renal technicians, and nutritionists — all working together to provide the best possible dialysis care.

St. Nicholas was the first private hospital in Nigeria to open a dialysis and kidney transplant centre — with over 350 successful transplants to date, representing 80% of all transplants in sub-Saharan Africa.

UK Visa TB Screening

St. Nicholas Hospital is a UKVI-approved tuberculosis testing centre. If you are applying for a UK visa and require a TB certificate, we conduct official screenings recognised by the UK Home Office.

The UK Government requires visa applicants from certain countries to undergo tuberculosis testing at an approved clinic before applying. Our results are issued within 5 working days and are fully recognised by UK Visas and Immigration.

The Process

01Book your appointment online or by phone
02Attend your screening at our Lagos Island branch (bring original passport)
03Chest X-ray is taken and reviewed by our specialist radiologist
04UKVI-recognised certificate issued within 5 working days

Cost: ₦25,000 per applicant. Families applying together may book together. Please bring your original passport on the day.
