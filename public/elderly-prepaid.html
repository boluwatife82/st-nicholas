The Reality

Aging in Nigeria
Comes with Real Risks

As our parents grow older, their health needs multiply — but the Nigerian healthcare system was not built to handle them proactively. By the time most families act, a health crisis is already underway. The question is: will you be ready?

The St. Nicholas Hospital Elderly Pre-Paid Medical Scheme was designed specifically for this reality — giving your parent consistent, expert medical attention before emergencies happen, not after.

Hypertension & Stroke
Over 30% of Nigerians above 60 live with uncontrolled hypertension — the leading cause of stroke and sudden death in older adults.
Delayed Emergency Response
In a medical emergency, the first 60 minutes decide outcomes. Without a plan, families lose critical time navigating hospitals, payments and paperwork.
Catastrophic Out-of-Pocket Costs
A single hospital admission for an elderly parent can cost ₦500,000 – ₦2,000,000+. Without a plan, families are left financially exposed at the worst possible moment.
No Dedicated Elderly Care
Most Nigerian hospitals treat elderly patients the same as everyone else — long queues, rushed consultations, no continuity. The elderly deserve better.

Routine Tests, X-Rays & Lab Work
Included

Prevention is far cheaper than cure. The scheme includes routine diagnostic testing — blood pressure monitoring, blood sugar checks, kidney function tests, chest X-rays, and other essential baseline investigations that track your parent's health over time.

Results are reviewed by your parent's dedicated physician and stored in their record. Any concerning change triggers a prompt consultation — catching problems early when they are most treatable. This is what separates managed care from a simple doctor visit.

Admissions & Minor Procedures
Covered

When your parent needs more than an outpatient visit, the scheme provides for hospital admissions and minor procedures within the plan. This means your parent is admitted quickly, without the stress of upfront payment negotiations or administrative delays at the ward level.

Minor procedures covered include wound care, dressings, minor surgical interventions, IV therapy and observation stays. Your dedicated physician oversees the admission and coordinates discharge planning with the family.
