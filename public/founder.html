Early Life & Education

Born to Lead

Dr. Moses Adekoyejo Majekodunmi was born on August 17, 1916, in Nigeria. His journey was defined from the outset by an unrelenting pursuit of excellence and an instinct for service to humanity.

He studied Medicine at Trinity College, University of Dublin, graduating in 1941 — becoming one of the youngest Africans at just twenty-six to achieve advanced postgraduate medical qualifications. His academic foundation extended beyond medicine; he also earned a degree in Logic and Philosophy, reflecting the depth of intellect that would shape every chapter of his extraordinary career.

Medical Career

A Career Built on Excellence

Upon returning to Nigeria, Dr. Majekodunmi served across government medical institutions before advancing his specialisation in Obstetrics and Gynaecology in Ireland. His expertise led him to key roles at Massey Street Maternity Hospital and General Hospital Lagos — where he became widely known for clinical precision and natural leadership.

"His expertise led him to the forefront of Nigerian medicine — not merely as a practitioner, but as a builder of institutions that would outlive him."

Statesman & Nation Builder

Medicine, Politics & Nation Building

Dr. Majekodunmi's influence extended far beyond the hospital ward. He was a defining figure in Nigeria's early political and healthcare architecture at the very moment the nation was finding its footing.

Federal Minister of Health (1961–1966)
Senator & Leader of the Senate
Administrator of the Western Region
Founded Lagos University Medical School
Co-founder, Lagos University Teaching Hospital

As Federal Minister of Health from 1961 to 1966, he championed bold policies that expanded access to healthcare across the country. He established critical institutions — most notably the Lagos University Medical School — and contributed to laying the foundation of the Lagos University Teaching Hospital (LUTH), cementing his legacy as a true nation builder.

His political career saw him serve as Senator, Leader of the Senate, and Administrator of the Western Region — a rare combination of medical and civic leadership that few in Nigerian history have matched.

The Hospital He Built

A Modest Beginning,
An Enduring Legacy

In 1952, Dr. Majekodunmi founded what is now St. Nicholas Hospital — beginning in a modest converted residence on Lagos Island, with a focus on obstetrics, gynaecology and paediatric care. The name itself reflects his values: St. Nicholas is the patron saint of women and children.

What started as a small, personal practice has grown — over more than seven decades — into one of Nigeria's most respected and recognised private hospitals, operating five branches across Lagos, performing over 350 kidney transplants, delivering Nigeria's first IVF, and operating the first private Oncology clinic in the country.

Every service we offer, every patient we treat, and every standard we uphold traces its roots back to the vision of one man who believed that Nigerians deserved world-class healthcare delivered with compassion, dignity and integrity.
