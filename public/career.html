Health & Safety

Domestic Staff Health Screening

St. Nicholas Hospital offers a dedicated Domestic Staff Screening Programme — an essential health assessment for food handlers, domestic workers, housekeepers, cooks, and other household staff.

Foodborne illnesses are usually infectious and toxic in nature, caused by bacteria, viruses, parasites, or chemical substances entering the body through contaminated food or water. Food handlers and domestic staff play a vital role in the spread of these diseases — making regular health screening critical for any household or establishment.

Our screening programme provides a comprehensive health assessment to ensure your domestic staff are in good health and pose no risk to your family or workplace. All results are handled with complete discretion and confidentiality.

Full Blood Work & Urinalysis

Comprehensive blood panel and urine tests to screen for infections, parasitic conditions, and general health status.

Typhoid & Hepatitis Screening

Testing for typhoid fever, Hepatitis A, B, and C — diseases commonly transmitted through contaminated food and water handling.

Stool Microscopy & Culture

Detection of intestinal parasites, bacteria, and pathogens that food handlers can unknowingly transmit to others.

Skin & Respiratory Check

Physical examination including skin condition and respiratory screening relevant to domestic and food service roles.

Certificate of Fitness Issued

Upon passing the screening, staff receive an official Certificate of Fitness — recognised by households, hotels, and food establishments.
